I am a third year Ph.D. student in Computing and Information Science at the Golisano College of Computing and Information Science, Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT) under the advisement of Prof. Matt Huenerfauth. Motivated in harnessing the learning capabilities of computers to empower people, my current research aims to inform the design and the evaluation of Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) technology for use in captioning for people who are deaf or hard of hearing (DHH). I am also interested in applying machine learning concepts to build accessbility tools for humans, that are validated through real-world studies and observations. Currently, my work explores the modeling of the understandability of spoken message for the various user-group (hearing and DHH users), in the presence of recognition errors from ASR systems.

Research Interests: Human-Computer Interaction, Accessiblity, Speech Processing, Natural Language Processing.
